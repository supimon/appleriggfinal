A private, family-owned business built on the foundations of a financial services group established in 1903, our goal is to nurture valuable and enduring businesses, drawing on our heritage and values whilst mindful of the responsibility to reinvent and innovate to be fit and relevant for the future.

Responsible stewardship is at the core of our approach to business. It requires us to focus on both preservation and growth. We respond to change using the skills, insights and long-term horizon afforded from being both a client and an owner, in order to shape an entrepreneurial strategy to meet the needs of the future.

Our rare perspective and approach represent an alignment of interests seldom seen in the financial services world.

"

Like people, businesses have finite lives. Family business ownership permits greater endurance as generational transfer injects a new lease of life or a change in directions. In some cases, the legacy of the family continues to make a positive impact that extends beyond the lifespan of the original firm.

Applerigg Chairman, Alex Scott

Our board

Alex Scott

- Executive Chairman, Applerigg, Sandaire and Lord North Street

- Non-Executive Chairman, Sandaire (Singapore)

- Non-Executive Director, Yealand Group

Alex is Chairman of Applerigg and a number of its subsidiary companies

Having overseen the sale of his family's Provincial Group businesses in 1994, Alex founded Sandaire in 1996 as a multi-family office to provide advice and investement services to his own family and to other families and foundations in similar situations.

He is a non-executive director of his family's investment holding company, and of several private companies. He is a trustee of the Grosvenor Estate, Chairman of Wheatsheaf Investments and of the Grosvenor pension plan.

A leading participant in the world of family businesses, Alex co-founded and is the Life-President of the Institute of Family Businesses (UK). He has several philanthrophical responsibilities and commitments.

Alex read Politics, Philosophy and Economics at Exeter College, Oxford and holds an MBA from IMD, Lausanne.
